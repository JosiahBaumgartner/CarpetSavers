Carpet Cleaning

First, we cover your carpets with a pre-treatment which loosens dirt and debris and prepares your carpets to be thoroughly cleaned.

We then use our top of the line Prochem Everest HP steam cleaner to dig deep into your carpets, removing all the dirt, grime and germs. Our truck mounted system allows us to fully extract the buildup from your carpets and remove it completely from your home without risk of spilling large reservoirs of disgusting water like rental machines or shop vacs.

Finally, we offer a protective treatment and/or deodorizing enzyme treatment. The application of a carpet protectant helps the carpets themselves last longer by making your carpets more resistant to stains, dirt and spills and our deodorizing treatment directly fights and breaks down odors from pets, kids and more!

Carpet Savers guarantees that we will do a far better job than any DIY or rented carpet cleaner. We will leave your home much cleaner and healthier than is possible with such cleaners.

Tile & Grout Cleaning

Using our special tile wand, we are able to clean the dirtiest of tile and grout! First we pretreat the tile and grout to allow the dirt and grime time to loosen up before our machine uses highly pressurized 250°F detergent strips away years of grime and dirt buildup.

We not only deep clean your grout, removing the buildup and sterilizing it, we can also re-seal your grout. Re-sealing the grout ensures a longer life for your floor and a longer lasting clean.

Air Duct Cleaning

Carpet Savers trusts only the RamAir advanced, closed circuit, air duct cleaning system to give your home the cleanest possible air ducts and dryer vents. The RamAir self-propelled jet hose moves quickly through your duct system blowing the dirt, dust and debris loose and backward as it passes. When retracted, the hose continues to blow back, forcing the junk in your ducts back with it.

The RamAir vent cover provides a sealed and closed exit point for the dust, dirt and other particles, allowing them to only get sucked into the vacuum hose and out of your home – nothing escapes into your home as we clean your air ducts.

Carpet Restretching

You’ve probably seen the traditional method of laying and stretching carpets, a knee kick pad. While this is an effective, industry standard method, Carpet Savers goes above and beyond to provide you with a superior, longer lasting, life time warrantied carpet stretch. We use a power stretcher to lock your carpet into its most snug and optimal position before trimming any excess carpet and reinstalling it.

One common issue with restretched carpet are creases that have developed in the carpet. Many lesser carpet stretchers can stretch and reset the carpet, but the crease can be left in the fibrous base just above the padding. Not so with Carpet Savers! After we power stretch and reset the carpet, we go can to to work with our Rotovac to completely eliminate the appearance of the crease, making your carpet tight, smooth and looking reborn.

Carpet Repair

One of the most common issues requiring carpets needing repair are pet damage issues. We love our furry friends, trust us we’ve got a few at Carpet Savers, but having an animal in the house can be less than kind to your carpets. Pets can dig or tear holes in the carpets, pull them up and chew on them or wreak many other forms of havoc on carpets. That’s where Carpet Savers comes in. Our expert workmen can do a patch repair on damaged carpet to restore it to its original look. And just like our stretching services, we guarantee and warranty the stability and hold of the patch for life.* Typically we can use carpet remnants to achieve this but if you don’t have remnants we can pull a sample from a closet or other hidden area. Once the repair is done we can help blend the replacement with a cleaning.

*While we guarantee and warranty the strength and stability of the patch, we cannot guarantee the look or blend. Unfortunately not all patches can look seamless due to differences such as level of wear or even slight color differences in carpet batches. Steam cleaning and the passage of time will usually help the patch to blend into a uniform look.  Most often, patching the carpet does in fact look better than damaged carpet, but we’ll give you an honest assessment and recommendation so you can make the best choice for your home.
